This is your unfair advantage. Nobody else in the AI education space is also flying King Airs and running an STR company. The combination of pilot + entrepreneur + AI builder is extremely memorable and creates instant credibility. Every piece of content should reinforce this identity.

The lifestyle content (yo-yo tricks especially) is a secret weapon for virality. A guy doing insane yo-yo tricks in a cockpit, then cutting to building AI agents, then wakesurfing — that's an algorithm magnet. Use these as hooks and transitions, not standalone content.

## Content Production System (5-7 hrs/week)

This is how to hit the cadence above without burning out:

### Weekly Batch Day (Saturday or Sunday — 3 hours)

**Hour 1: Film**
- Record 1 long-form build video (screen record your Claude Code / n8n session)
- Film 2-3 quick talking-head clips for Reels/Shorts
- Grab any cockpit/pilot footage during the week on your phone

**Hour 2: Edit + Clip**
- Rough edit the long-form video (cut dead air, add chapters)
- Pull 3-5 Short/Reel clips from the long-form
- Create 1-2 carousel graphics (use Canva templates)

**Hour 3: Write + Schedule**
- Write captions for all posts (use a consistent voice — direct, no fluff, real results)
- Schedule everything for the week using Later, Buffer, or native scheduling
- Write YouTube titles, descriptions, tags

### Weekday Micro-Tasks (15 min/day — 1.5 hours total)

- Reply to every comment and DM (engagement drives algorithm)
- Post 1-2 Stories (phone selfie, quick behind-the-scenes, poll)
- Engage on 10 posts in your niche (AI builders, pilots, STR operators) — leave real comments, not "great post!"
- Share any Skool community wins to Stories

### Jam Session Days (Already happening)
- Hit record on Zoom — this is content
- After the session, clip the best 2-3 moments for Shorts/Reels
- Post a recap carousel: "Top 3 things we built in today's jam session"

## Growth Tactics (Beyond Just Posting)

### 1. Collaborate with Other AI Builders
Find 5-10 creators in the AI agent / no-code automation space on YouTube and Instagram. Propose:
- Guest on their channel to show your stack
- Invite them to a jam session and post the collab
- Cross-promote Skool communities

Target creators with 1K-50K followers — they're hungry for collabs and your pilot angle makes you memorable.

### 2. Leverage the Lifestyle Angle Everywhere
This is your hook. Every thumbnail, every first frame of a Reel, every carousel cover should make someone stop scrolling. "Wait — is this guy in a cockpit talking about AI agents?"

Use cockpit footage, yo-yo tricks, wakesurfing clips, and action shots as B-roll in EVERY video intro. Even for pure tech content. It's your brand signature.

**Viral transition content ideas:**
- Yo-yo trick → catches it → "anyway, here's the AI agent I built today" (cut to screen)
- Cockpit landing → walks to desk → opens Claude Code
- Wakesurfing wipeout → "my first n8n flow also crashed like this" → shows the working version
- Golf swing → "automating my business so I can do more of this"

The yo-yo tricks alone could carry a viral Reel. A guy doing competition-level yo-yo tricks who also builds AI agents and flies planes? That's inherently shareable content.

### 3. SEO-Driven YouTube Titles
People are actively searching for:
- "how to build AI agents"
- "claude code tutorial"
- "n8n automation examples"
- "local LLM setup mac"
- "AI for Airbnb"

Title your videos to match these searches. This is how you get discovered by people who will pay $67/mo.

### 4. Repurpose Jam Sessions Aggressively
You're already doing live sessions — this is a goldmine most creators waste. Every jam session should produce:
- 1 YouTube video (edited replay)
- 3-5 Shorts/Reels (best moments)
- 1 carousel (recap/highlights)
- 2-3 Stories (clips + "you missed this" FOMO)

### 5. Weekly "Build Request" Engagement
Post a weekly Story poll or post: "What should I automate next?" or "Drop your business — I'll show you what AI agent you need."

This drives comments (algorithm loves it), gives you content ideas, and positions you as the guy who solves real problems.

## The Non-Negotiable Rules

1. **Post consistently or don't post at all.** 4x/week on IG, 1 long-form + 3 Shorts on YT. Every week. No gaps.
2. **Every post has a CTA.** No orphan content. Everything points somewhere — YouTube, Skool, or DMs.
3. **Lead with the pilot angle.** It's your hook. Use it in thumbnails, first frames, and intros. Always.
4. **Show real results.** "This agent saved me 10 hours/week on guest messages" beats "AI is the future" every time.
5. **Engage more than you post.** 15 min/day replying to comments and engaging on other accounts. The algorithm rewards conversation.
6. **Batch create, don't daily grind.** One 3-hour session produces a week of content. Protect that block.
7. **Repurpose everything.** 1 YouTube video = 4 Shorts + 2 Reels + 1 carousel + 5 Stories. Never create content that only lives in one place.
